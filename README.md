# Pakistan Ride-Hailing Market Analysis 2025

## Overview
Independent market research analyzing Uber, Careem, inDrive, and Bykea across Karachi. Based on primary survey data (N=113 respondents) and manual fare benchmarking (120 data points across 5 routes and 3 time periods).

## Key Findings

**Market Share:**
- inDrive: 54.87% (62 users) — Market leader despite being newest entrant
- Careem: 22.12% (25 users)
- Uber: 11.50% (13 users)
- Bykea: 9.73% (11 users)

**Pricing Insights:**
- Bykea cheapest: PKR 305.22 average fare
- Uber most expensive: PKR 475.22 average fare
- 55.8% price spread indicates market fragmentation
- Careem has highest surge rate at 33.50% (supply constraint signal)

**User Behavior:**
- Price is #1 switching driver (35/113 respondents)
- 50%+ of users ride 3-4 times per week or more
- 48% of users switched apps in last 6 months
- inDrive leads in app experience satisfaction (4.06/5.00)

**Strategic Insight:** inDrive wins by balancing low prices WITH high satisfaction — no trade-off needed.

## Methodology

**Primary Research:**
- Survey: 113 respondents via Google Forms
- Demographics: Karachi urban users, ages 18-45
- Questions: 10-question survey on app usage, satisfaction, switching behavior
- Fare Testing: 5 routes × 4 apps × 3 time slots = 60+ fare data points
- Collection period: June 2025

**Secondary Research:**
- GSMA Mobile Economy Pakistan reports
- State Bank of Pakistan digital payments data
- Company press releases and news coverage

## Key Insights & Recommendations

1. **Price leadership alone doesn't win** — Bykea is cheapest but not market leader. inDrive wins by combining price + quality.

2. **Supply problems = customer loss** — Careem's high surge rate (33.50%) signals driver supply issues. This hurts competitive position.

3. **No incumbent lock-in** — 48% switching rate in 6 months means any player can gain share with right positioning.

4. **Market is mature** — 50%+ riding 3+ times weekly means this is essential commute infrastructure, not occasional luxury.
